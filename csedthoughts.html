Thoughts on CS Education

When I first learned computer science and programming, it felt like a game changer to me. It was a way for me to create things through writing code. It was a powerful tool I could use to aid me in various parts of my life. I feel like I've been able to do a lot with my computer science knowledge and I want to be able to give students the same feeling and opportunity. I want to show students how much they could potentially do with computer science knowledge. I love seeing and hearing about what students want to do or make and helping them reach their goals.

I believe that computer science deserves to be taught on its own, and I appreciate that computer science classes are becoming more common throughout the state. However, I also think that computer science could be integrated into other subjects as well and in fact I hope to show this to my future students as well. Whether it's creating concrete poetry using text formatting, making interactive historical timelines as a website, building simulations to explore scientific concepts, or whatever else, there is a way that students can integrate computer science into their favorite subjects and vice-versa. Plus, the logical thinking that one develops while learning computer science can be applied in many situations.

I think that everybody deserves the chance to learn computer science and there's something to gain for everyone taking such classes. Some students may appreciate what they can create with computer science. Other students may see computer science as a worthwhile potential future career path. Even if students don't plan on doing anything with computer science, computer science knowledge is important in understanding the world around us as computer-based technology continues to become bigger parts of our lives.
